1/4: walked into the kitchen this morning and saw a pile of ants. one time nathan came over and the whole time i was sprinkling cinnamon on ants hoping they would go away. a lot of childhood memories have been flitting through my head lately.

12/31: my least favorite question of the year: 'how are you?''

12/25: today was the beginning of the first family road trip i can remember that didn't involve someone yelling. also finally found the moon and the slopes aren't nearly as packed as they were a year ago.

12/22: i wish the world made less sense. (not that it makes sense.)

12/21: i dread coming back to benicia. not because i don't want to but because i feel stuck here. public transportation is almost non-existent and all the homes on my street look identical. it looks like the fucking american dream over here: nice house, nice car, nice kids, nice dog, nice park, nice school, everything is so fucking nice it's awful, while the lighting in my room has that gross washed out tint of flourescent lights which reminds me of that painting of the diner. these houses don't even look good. everyone's obsessed with efficiency. i suppose there's more money in developing whole neighborhoods rather than individual lots but what happened to authenticity. i miss maryland. i miss brick and wood. i miss old neighborhoods where the trees have had time to shatter the sidewalks with their roots. i miss my home. i wish everything wasn't so plastic and shiny and perfect and new and shadowless.

12/20: nothing lifts my mood like rain and the mundane. i write these because my memory is awful. i can't seem to remember anything unless i'm reminded of it first. it doesn't make sense. i started keeping track of everything i did this semester in a notebook: problem sets, essays, coffee/lunch/dinner dates, office hours, everything. i'll write in the designated column of the day, hangout with eric, but what we really did was walk to indian rock and watch the sunset surrounded by strangers and the smell of piss. a couple making out. someone smoking weed. a father and his children on a picnic and we wondered where their mother was. another couple must have been on their first date. the girl was quite disillusioned with postgrad life and the boy readily agreed. the sky looked like it could be a rothko painting, blue and orange. i wanted to lean into eric but at that moment at least i was too self-conscious. i'm quite surprised by how much i can remember. we walked back to my studio in the dark and i offered eric some chocolate pretzels. tiff had gone home that weekend. this was almost two months ago. there are more details left unsaid: the rock sat next to a house like neighbors, we came across 'doctor no' written on the sidewalk -- a james bond reference, i picked a lemon and then dropped it, we left early because eric was hungry, we passed a street by a fountain where several nobel winners live, and so the list goes. i can recall the sensations and feelings but i can only write them down in this factual way and hope that in reading words become more.

12/19: i've been thinking about the future recently. i'd like to try my hand at gardening. i think i'd enjoy it quite a bit.

on 12/17: went for a walk at night and came across these shadows. one tree shade was sprouting velvet from its leaves. a stray cat let me pick her up and i almost got lost. it was much warmer in the hills than on campus. there's a long staircase on the west side of the hills with houses on either side. i sat in the shadows half way up and watched a woman climbing up and down the stairs below unloading groceries from her car. she didn't noticed me watching her. no one ever looks up. i would like to sleep there one day. it was quite warm and the lights were pretty. people enter their homes and don't come out again at night. i'm slowing losing the urge to smoke.
