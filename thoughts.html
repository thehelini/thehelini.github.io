12/20: nothing lifts my mood like rain and mundane life. i write these because when it comes to non-quantitative facts my memory is awful. i started keeping track of everything i did this semester in a notebook: problem sets, essays, coffee/lunch/dinner dates, office hours, everything. just reading the bare facts of what i did that day reminds me of the circumstances around each event. i'll write in the designated column of the day, hangout with eric, but what we really did that day was walk to indian rock and watch the sunset surrounded by strangers and the smell of piss. a couple was making out. someone was smoking weed. a father took his children on a picnic and i wondered where their mother was. one couple must have been on their first date. the girl was quite disillusioned with postgrad life and the boy readily agreed. the sky looked like it could be a rothko painting, blue and orange. i wanted to lean into eric but at that moment at least i was too self-conscious. i'm quite surprised by how much i can remember when given the right ques and madeleines. we walked back to my studio in the dark and i offered eric some chocolate pretzels. tiff had gone home that weekend. there is so much more i'm not writing down. this was almost two months ago. i can recall the sensations and feelings but i can only write them down in this factual way and hope that in reading the words become more again.

12/19: i've been thinking about the future recently. i'd like to try my hand at gardening. i think i'd enjoy it quite a bit.


on 12/17: went for a walk at night and came across these shadows. one tree shade was sprouting velvet from its leaves. a stray cat let me pick her up and i almost got lost. it was much warmer in the hills than on campus. there's a long staircase on the west side of the hills with houses on either side. i sat in the shadows half way up and watched a woman climbing up and down the stairs below unloading groceries from her car. she didn't noticed me watching her. i would like to sleep there one day. it was quite warm and the lights were pretty. people enter their homes and don't come out again at night. i'm slowing losing the urge to smoke.
